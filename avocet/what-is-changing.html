What's changing?

HEFCE has a new policy for Open Access in the next Research Excellence Framework (REF).

The policy requires you to submit your accepted, peer-reviewed articles and conference proceedings to an institutional or subject repository.

We will continue to advise on other funders' policies and to pay eligible Open Access costs for papers funded by Research Councils UK (RCUK) and The Charity Open Access Fund, which includes the Wellcome Trust.

Upload your accepted manuscripts and we’ll make sure your work is eligible for the next REF, and guide you through how to comply with other funder requirements.

What type of manuscripts are affected?

All accepted journal articles and conference proceedings (with an ISSN) should be uploaded, to ensure they are eligible for the next REF.

This policy does not apply to monographs, book chapters or other long-form publications, working papers, creative or practice-based research outputs or data.

When do I need to do something?

As soon as your peer-reviewed manuscript has been accepted upload it. HEFCE policy requires manuscripts to be submitted within 3 months of the acceptance date.

This policy comes into force on 1 April 2016, yet we want to ensure this shift is managed sooner rather than later, so that no research is omitted.

Uploading at acceptance lets us check your options and advise you how to fill in copyright or Open Access forms to comply with other funders’ requirements, and – in eligible cases – to commit funds to cover Open Access fees.

Research Excellence Framework (REF)

By uploading your peer-reviewed manuscript as soon as it is accepted by a publisher, we’ll make sure you comply with HEFCE’s Open Access policy for the next REF and deposit it in the institutional repository.

Publisher embargoes are respected (the period when you can only access an article with a paid access). Manuscripts must be free to read and download after the following periods:

  • 12 months for REF Main Panel A and REF Main Panel B
  • 24 months for REF Main Panel C and REF Main Panel D7

How does this apply to other funders?

In addition to the HEFCE policy, other funders’ Open Access policies may apply to your manuscript.

When you upload your manuscript we'll check which policies apply and guide you through how to make it compliant.

The University manages two Open Access funds:

  • The Charity Open Access Fund (includes Wellcome Trust)
  • Research Councils UK

We can pay Open Access charges for eligible articles, from these funds.
