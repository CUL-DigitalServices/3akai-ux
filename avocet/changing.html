What's changing?

HEFCE policy is changing and it impacts how your research and scholarly output is eligible for the next REF. The new policy requires articles and conference proceedings to be available in an open access repository if they are to be eligible.

RCUK, Wellcome and other funders also have various policies concerning the deposit of research output.

By uploading your work and using this service, we will help ensure you meet REF and funder policy requirements.

We are committed to enabling your research to flourish while giving you the choice to publish where you want.

Eligibility for REF

The new open access policies established by HEFCE will impact on the eligibility of your work for the next REF. The funders’ policies, including RCUK and The Wellcome Trust, impact on future access to funding.

HEFCE’s current position is that from April 2014 any research to be submitted as a unit of assessment to REF 2020 should be available as open access at the time of publication.

HEFCE define open access as peer-reviewed manuscripts to be freely accessible at the time of publication.

In 2013, HEFCE conducted a consultation to explore how to make open access a requirement for all research. It is expected this will enforce the upload of manuscript’s to an institutional repository, like this one. Our work to establish this system is to pre-empt and prepare researchers for this policy enforcement.

Upload your accepted manuscripts to meet this requirement and make sure you are compliant with both REF, your funder’s and publisher’s open access policies.

Other Funder Requirements

RCUK

In line with government policy from 1 April 2013 all articles arising from research funded by RCUK must be made available under open access.

Read more

The Wellcome Trust

Wellcome Trust Grant Conditions require that all research papers funded in whole or in part through the Europe PubMed Central (Europe PMC) or PubMedcentral (PMC) should be available with an electronic copy in a repository as soon as possible or within six months of publication. This applies to all research papers that have been submitted and accepted for publication in a peer reviewed journal.

For further guidance

What type of publications are affected?

All journal articles and conference proceedings should be open access at the time of publication, to ensure they are eligible for the next REF.

When do I need to do something?

When your manuscript has been peer reviewed and accepted by the journal, upload it here.

The HEFCE policy has been in force since March 2014. HEFCE is planning a two year transition period before their policy is enforced by 2016. Yet at The University we want to ensure this shift is managed sooner rather than later to ensure no research is omitted.

When you upload your manuscript you will be guided through the process making it as simple and clear as possible.
