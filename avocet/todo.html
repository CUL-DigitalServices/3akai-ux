What do I need to do?

Deposit a manuscript

When a journal accepts your manuscript for publication you need to upload it here.

Which manuscripts do I need to upload?

Upload articles or contributions to conference proceedings as soon as they are accepted by the journal. Monographs, books and other book chapters do not need to be uploaded.

Which version should I upload?

You should upload your Author Accepted Manuscript, sometimes referred to as the Author’s Final Version. This is your version of the manuscript after peer review and including any final changes, but it is likely not to have been typeset or copy-edited by a publisher.

What formats do you accept?

We accept any file format including Word, Pages and PDF. We will ensure each manuscript uploaded to the repository is converted to a PDF file.

We are currently only able to accept one file per upload. For example, a manuscript in LaTeX format is comprised of multiple files, therefore a LaTeX file will need to be converted to a PDF before it can be uploaded. We plan to support multiple files in future.

Copyright

When you return your edited manuscript to your publisher you are asked to sign a Copyright Transfer Agreement. This is standard in most traditional publication agreements. It is important for you to retain rights as an author enabling you to publish your work open access, use it in teaching materials and to promote your research work i.e., via your website. As the publishing landscape shifts it is important that you know how your work might be affected.

Why might you want to retain copyright?

A traditional publisher’s agreement, which sees you transfer copyright to the publisher, might affect your eligibility for the next REF which requires work to be open access at the time of publication. A traditional copyright agreement may affect your ability to share your work online.

Retaining copyright, or adding an addendum which allows you to comply with funders’ open access policies will ensure your work is eligible. It will also help you reach a wider audience and have more control over how the distribution of your work online.

Your Copyright Options

By uploading your Author Accepted Manuscripts we will be able to ensure your work is compliant with funder policies as well as making it eligible for the next REF.

Read more

Your Author Accepted Manuscript will be uploaded to the institutional repository and will be embargoed until at least the publication date. Unless you agree otherwise, your manuscript will not be open access immediately. Instead we will contact you to confirm how you would like us to proceed once the publisher’s embargo period is ended. If we cannot contact you we will publish your manuscript in accordance with the funder policy to ensure it is eligible for REF.

The University will not publish, distribute or modify your work until after the funder’s embargo expires without your permission.

If you have any questions or need clarification please contact us.

You have two copyright options.

Use an Author Addendum

Modify your contract with your publisher to include a caveat covering digital usage and access.

The SPARC Author Addendum enables you to change the traditional agreement you have with your publisher to allow you to retain key rights in relation to your work. You still transfer copyright, but keep some rights back for yourself, allowing you an element of control over how and where you share your work.

Sign a traditional copyright agreement

A CTA (Copyright Transfer Agreement) will give your publisher copyright but might affect how and when you can make your manuscript open access.

Read more

If you sign a traditional copyright agreement with a publisher, often described as a CTA, you are signing up to the publisher’s terms and conditions. We will need to check whether your publisher offers an adequate model for open access, thus ensuring your article is eligible for REF.

Your rights will vary depending on your publisher but if you sign a CTA you may need to request permission to;

  • Upload your manuscript to the institutional repository
  • Publish your manuscript on your website
  • Share your manuscript on Academia.edu and Research Gate
  • Distribute your article to colleagues and students
  • Include your articles in course materials
  • Make modifications to your article

If you would like us to check your publisher’s CTA and offer you help and guidance on your rights then please contact us. We can also provide you with an overview of how your CTA affects your eligibility for REF.

What to do if you’ve already signed an agreement

If you have already entered into a traditional publisher’s contract but may need to arrange a retrospective amendment.

Read more

If you have already signed the publisher’s CTA then your rights may vary. If your publisher does not offer a Gold open access model then your manuscript may not be eligible for REF.

You may need to request permission from your publisher retrospectively if you need to;

  • Upload your manuscript to the institutional repository
  • Publish your manuscript on your website
  • Share your manuscript on Academia.edu and Research Gate
  • Distribute your article to colleagues and students
  • Include your articles in course materials
  • Make modifications to your article

We can contact your publisher and discover whether they are open access compliant, helping you to find out whether your manuscript will be eligible for REF. If your article is not yet published we can support you in negotiation for amended rights with your publisher. We may, or may not be able to upload your manuscript in the repository. If we can we will cite the journal as copyright holder.

I need to pay a publisher’s fee

If you have already opted for a “pay to publish” model you may need to pay an Article Processing Charge, or APC. Depending on the funder, the University can support you in covering this publishing fee. We can check your publication options and advise you what to do. The University’s preferred option for open access is to use a self-archiving University of Cambridge Repository which requires you to upload your manuscript.

Open access funds are available at the University, funded by RCUK and The Wellcome Trust. This support enables researchers to comply with open access criteria. Both funding bodies have set conditions linked to the use of a publisher’s fee.

The University can pay for colour and page charges for RCUK funded articles only. The University cannot pay for image costs. These are the responsibility of the author.

If you have any queries or require guidance then please contact us.

Acknowledge your funder

You will need to acknowledge your funder when your manuscript is published. When you upload your manuscript, you will be able to add the details of your funder.

Read more

The Research Information Network has developed a standard acknowledgment which is acceptable to all funders. Acknowledgements should follow these guidelines;

  • the funding agency name should be written in full
  • the grant number should be in square brackets
  • multiple grant numbers should be separated by a comma and space
  • funding agencies should be separated by a semi-colon.

eg. 'This work was supported by the Wellcome Trust [grant number], [grant number]; Medical Research Council [grant number] and Cancer Research UK [grant number]
If you have any queries or require guidance then please contact us at XX.

Full details of the RIN guidelines can be found at http://rinarchive.jisc-collections.ac.uk/our-work/research-funding-policy-and-guidance/acknowledgement-funders-journal-articles

I’m publishing Gold open access, why do I have to upload here?

With different funder and publication policies impacting on open access, the University has opted for the self-archiving method. HEFCE policy requires articles and conference proceedings to be available in an open access repository if they are to be eligible. To reduce confusion and to maintain and ensure compliance with funder policy we would recommend you upload your manuscript to ensure it is eligible for the next REF. The university can support you with some publisher’s fees.

My manuscript is on ArXiv, why do I have to upload it here?

To be eligible for the next REF your research needs to comply with funder policies, as well as publishers. HEFCE policy requires peer reviewed articles and conference proceedings to be available in an open access repository if they are to be eligible. Articles on ArXiv are generally pre-print and not peer reviewed.

My manuscript is on PubMed Central, why do I have to upload it here?

To be eligible for the next REF your research needs to comply with funder policies, as well as publishers. HEFCE policy requires articles and conference proceedings to be available in an open access repository if they are to be eligible.

I’m not eligible for the REF at the moment. Why should I care?

The Research Excellence Framework (REF) is the system for assessing the quality of research in UK higher education institutions (HEIs). This assessment is vital for the funding both of researchers and the university. To allow as much work as possible to be eligible for the next REF, all articles should be made open access.
